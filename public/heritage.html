Heritage: Uncover traditional history

Taj Mahal , Agra

Emperor Shah Jahan constructed the Taj Mahal, a white marble work of Mughal architecture and one of the Seven Wonders of the World, as a memorial to his wife, Mumtaj Mahal.It is referred to as the "Jewel of Muslim Art in India" and is regarded as the best example of Mughal architecture in existence. Probably the most well-known monument in the world due to its affiliation with Indian historical sites is this one.

Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh

The well-known Indian UNESCO World Heritage site Khajuraho is situated in the state of Madhya Pradesh. Situated 175 miles southeast of Jhansi, Khajuraho is a singular cultural site that is well-known for a collection of Hindu and Jain temples.It is composed of eighty-five Temples dispersed over twenty square km. Of all these temples, the most well-known is the Kandariya temple.

Hampi, Karnataka

Situated in the northern region of Karnataka, Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage site.The superb Dravidian architectural and artistic style is exemplified by the Hampi ruins, a group of heritage monuments. The Virupaksa Temple, which is still a major Hindu religious centre, is the most important heritage landmark at this location. This historical site is home to a number of additional monuments that are generally referred to as the "Group of Monuments at Hampi.

Ajanta Caves, Maharashtra

The Ajanta Caves, one of the first World Heritage sites in India, are home to 31 exquisitely crafted Buddhist cave monuments, paintings, and sculptures that span from the second century BCE to 650 CE.It is possible to argue that this is the start of Indian classical art because it mostly consists of sculptures and carvings depicting Buddha's life.

Ellora Caves, Maharashtra

The Ellora Caves are an archaeological monument and a well-known World Heritage site in India.The existence of Jain, Buddhist, and Hindu temples and sculptures illustrates the ancient Indian society's tolerance for a diversity of faiths and beliefs. Charanandri Hills, Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain rock-cut temples, Viharas, and maths from the fifth and tenth centuries are all found at the excavated site.

Bodh Gaya, Bihar

Situated approximately 96 kilometres from Patna, Bihar, Bodh Gaya is an Indian heritage site officially recognised by UNESCO. The location holds great religious significance for Buddhists, as it is the site of Mahatma Buddha's enlightenment. Siddhartha attained enlightenment and became Gautam Buddha at the sacred Bodhi Tree.

Sun Temple, Konark, Odisha

The Sun Temple at Konark, located in Odisha, India, is a 13th-century marvel of Kalinga architecture dedicated to the Hindu sun god Surya.A UNESCO World Heritage Site, it stands as a testament to the architectural and artistic prowess of ancient India, attracting visitors from around the world for its historical significance and breathtaking beauty.

Red Fort, Delhi

The Red Fort was constructed by Emperor Shah Jahan when he moved his capital from Agra to Delhi, or what was then known as Shahjahanabad, and is located in the heart of the historic city of New Delhi. It is constructed of red sandstone and is home to a number of lesser structures, including the Diwan-i-aam, the Diwan-i-Khas, and the private pavilions. One of India's most well-known UNESCO World Heritage Sites is the Red Fort.

Sanchi, Madhya Pradesh

The Buddhist Monuments, the oldest stone structures in India, are found in Sanchi, Madhya Pradesh.The stupa underwent renovations during the Satavahana Period, when the gateways and railing were constructed and painted, after being vandalised in the second century BCE. There are a number of additional buildings here besides the Sanchi stupa, including temples, palaces, monasteries, and monolithic pillars. Up to the 12th century AD, it was a significant Buddhist pilgrimage place.

Chola Temples, Tamil Nadu

During the Chola Empire's control, these temples were constructed in the southern region of India. The three most significant of these temples are the Brihadisvara Temple, the Gangaikonda Cholapuram Temple, and the Airavatesvara Temple.The architectural magnificence and beauty of Chola art, architecture, and sculpture are attested to by these temples.
